# MAXCOOL — Solar-Powered Off-Grid Cooler

**Freedom that refreshes.**  
Autonomous cooling unit for off-grid 
communities in Mozambique and Africa.


## What is MAXCOOL?

MAXCOOL is a portable solar-powered refrigeration 
unit designed for informal markets, fishing 
communities, and off-grid health clinics in 
Mozambique. It operates 100% independently from 
the grid, requiring no external electricity and 
no ice.

Built for micro-entrepreneurs, families, and 
community health workers who lose food, income, 
and medicine daily due to lack of reliable cooling.


## The Problem

- 30 to 40% of perishable food is lost daily in 
  off-grid markets
- Families spend USD 31 to 47 monthly on ice
- Health clinics lose vaccines and medicines 
  during power outages and climate shocks
- 590 million+ Africans live without grid access


## The Solution

An autonomous cooling unit that:

- Generates cold internally via DC compressor
- Powered by integrated flexible solar panels (100W+)
- LiFePO4 battery backup (3.2V cells) for up to 10 hours of autonomy without sunlight
- Temperature range: -10°C to +15°C
- Zero external ice or electricity required
- Capacity: 30L (initial model)
- Target starting price: USD 290


## Current Technical Status

- Physical MVP validated in Maputo, Mozambique
- Achieved -5°C under 100% solar power in

- 15 hours battery autonomy
